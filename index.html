Introduction

I am a master's student in the Department of Biomedical Engineering at the Johns Hopkins Whiting School of Enginering and a Graduate Research Assistant in the Department of Biostatistics at the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health. My thesis work is on the Open Case Studies project from the Johns Hopkins Data Science Lab advised by Dr. Carrie Wright. Open Case Studies is a repository of open source data science case studies on current public health problems using real datasets. My project is updating the current case studies by removing barriers, implementing interactive elements, and expanding their reach. My master's thesis will analyze the impact of these updates on data science education inside and outside the classroom.

I find the brain fascinating and want to further our understanding of it. I am excited by the discoveries made possible by computational models and analyses in neuroscience and want to contribute to these pursuits. Through my course I've focused my studies on neuroengineering and biomedical data science. The classes I've taken in these areas have allowed me to practice wrangling, analyzing, and visualizing real biological data such as brain images, neural recordings, covid-19 cases and more. Post-graduation, I hope to gain industry experience working as a data scientist in biotech, ideally with neurological data. Later on, I plan to pursue a Ph.D in computational neuroscience.
